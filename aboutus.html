About Us

Our mission is to support school leaders and teachers in order to educate a more vibrant, stronger and more ardent generation of students to meet the challenges of the new rapid technologically developing age through STEM education.

We believe that educating all our younger generation will bring about innovative changes in the development of our nation both in technology and in moral conduct in the future.


We also operate globally reaching out to prospective students who want to study abroad. We render end to end services in visa assistance, travel assistance and admissions at the undergraduate and postgraduate levels along with tutorial services for SAT or GRE exams thus making us a one stop shop for overseas education.



What is STEM Education

STEM is an educational reform that has been in the making for years. It may simply be defined as the integration of science, technology, engineering, and mathematics into a new cross-disciplinary subject in schools. The study of STEM offers students the opportunity to make sense of the integrated world we live in rather than learning fragmented bits and pieces of knowledge and practices about it.



Why STEM Education

At the college and university levels, there are major concerns that the U.S. is loosing its technology and engineering leadership to other countries in the world. The Aerospace Industries Association of America reported in 2008 that the U.S. is currently experiencing a chronic decline in homegrown STEM talent, and is increasingly dependent on foreign scholars to fill the workforce and leadership voids Similarly in 2007, the total number of foreign citizens studying in the U.S. (including undergraduates) has passed the half-million mark. Nearly one-third of all graduate students enrolled in the U.S. universities now come from abroad (The National Academies, 2007)



Our 21st century learners need to learn the skills needed to meet the demands of today’s fast growing technology in today's classrooms by focusing on STEM education (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Math)

Leadership Team



Femi Adebanjo is currently a teacher of Mathematics with New York City Department of Education and an Adjunct Professor at the City University of Technology, Brooklyn, New York, with over 23 years of experience. Born and raised in Lagos, Nigeria, Femi Adebanjo attended Mayflower School, Ikenne and obtained B.Sc. Physics at the University of Lagos in 1985. He had brief employment with the Ministry of Education, Abeokuta, Nigeria as Inspector of Education. His major responsibility was facilitating on-site school visits and assessment in conjunction with Principal Managers.

He migrated to the United States in 1990 with a deep passion for computer science and relentless pursuit of a career in education. He obtained an M.A. in Mathematics Education at New York University, New York and currently in pursuant of a PhD degree in Leadership in Educational Policy and Administration. Notable accomplishment includes being a Mathematics Coach for Intermediate School teachers for 4 years and a member of a Mathematics Curriculum Review and Development Committee charged with the task of developing mathematics curriculum that would suit the need of at-risk students so they can be as successful in their academic career as their more advanced counterparts.

Femi facilitated school-based Professional Developments on integrating technology into classroom learning and teaching techniques and best practices.

He is a member of the American Educational Research Association, with a special interest in curriculum development and Educational Research Project.


He also holds membership of the National (American) Board for Professional Teaching Standards and the Independent Educational Consultants Association
